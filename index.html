In this part I will tell you a bit about my work experience. Let´s say I skip all the summer jobs in florist´s, pubs and camps and pounce directly to my main (and one and only) job. I work as development technologist in Meopta - Optika s. r. o. Well, technically my position is called thin layers desinger, but since noone (except some nerds maybe..) can imagine what that means, I decided to stick with development technologist. But since I already brought that up... Thin layers designer is a person, who desings thin layers (WOW! Right?) But seriously.. Thin layers - meaning really thin, like in the nanometer range, are manufactured to improve both physical and endurance properties. Thin layer, or thin film, can for example make an optical part more reflective or more scratch resistant. As the most commont thing you can think of is for example a mirror or glasses. I personally work on a bit more complicated layers - for IR light. Layers for this part of spectrum are more thick - meaning we are now in micrometers. The main use of layers for IR is probably for an army purposes. And because of that, I can´t really tell you more about my job.. :)
For those who want to learn more about this field, check auntie Wiki.

Anyway... as time went on, I realized more and more, that even though my job is pretty interesting, I miss creating codes.
